# Gym Buddy — Vision Narrative

*A ~2-minute script for a video, voice-over, or reading aloud. Designed to be shown to friends — skeptical and casual alike — to sell the vision before the app is built. Everything depicted here is technically buildable with 2026-era iOS + Apple Watch + AirPods Pro hardware. No sci-fi, no hand-waving.*

Total runtime: ~2 minutes at conversational pace (~270 words of dialogue/narration + beats).

---

## Format

| SCENE | VISUAL | AUDIO |
|---|---|---|

Scene numbers are cut points, not screen titles.

---

### 1. COLD OPEN — 15 sec

| VISUAL | AUDIO |
|---|---|
| Wide, quiet. An empty gym at first light. Dumbbells on a rack. A bench. Dust in a beam of sun. Hold. | **Narrator** (calm, not corporate): "The best personal trainers in the world charge two hundred dollars an hour. They keep their own hours. They only take a handful of clients. *If* you work with one — really work with one — you get stronger than you thought you could. Almost no one gets to." |

---

### 2. THE MORNING — 20 sec

| VISUAL | AUDIO |
|---|---|
| Close on a hand tying a running shoe. Pull back — a person in their living room, morning light. Apple Watch on wrist. An AirPod visible in the ear. | *Soft chime from the AirPods.* |
| Hold on the face. Not speaking. Listening. | **Coach** (warm, human, through the AirPods — audible to us): "Morning. Before you head in — your recovery's been off this week and your sleep last night was short. I've pulled today's volume back fifteen percent and cut the tempo work. You'll still hit the main lifts. You just won't be cooked after." |
| Small nod. Grabs keys. Out the door. | **Person** (just to themselves): "Yeah. That's fair." |

---

### 3. THE MOMENT — 45 sec

| VISUAL | AUDIO |
|---|---|
| Home gym or public gym. Phone on a tripod in the corner, or nowhere visible at all. They set up on a bench. Dumbbells in hand. | *Ambient gym sound. No music.* |
| Start of the set. Clean, controlled reps. | **Coach** (quiet, in rhythm): "One. … Two. … Three." |
| Rep 4. Rep 5. Rep 6. The pace is steady. | **Coach:** "Four. … Five. … Six." |
| Rep 7 — the bar slows on the way up. You see it on the face. The grind. | **Coach** (a half-beat before the sticking point, not after): "Drive — **one more** — push." |
| The rep grinds out. They rack the weight. Breathing hard. | **Coach** (softer, warm): "That's the one you weren't going to do alone." |
| Beat. They sit up, wipe their face. | **Person:** "Hey — left shoulder's a bit cranky." |
| | **Coach:** "Like three weeks ago? Want to swap overhead press for incline dumbbell next round? Takes the load off the AC joint." |
| | **Person:** "Yeah." |
| Quick cut to a phone screen: the plan updates. One line changes. Back to the athlete. | **Coach:** "Rest ninety seconds. Then we go again." |

---

### 4. WHAT IT BECOMES — 25 sec

| VISUAL | AUDIO |
|---|---|
| Montage, tight cuts, same person across weeks. Different outfits, same gym. A PR hit. A morning where they laugh at something the coach said. A rest day where they're on the couch and the coach checks in. A friend walks past their phone at the gym and you can hear the coach in the background — they look up, surprised. | **Narrator:** "It isn't a workout app. It's a coach. It watches. It remembers. It knows you slept five hours last Tuesday. It knows your left knee clicks on deep squats. It knows you hate tempo work and love AMRAPs." |
| Hold on the athlete, walking out of the gym. Dusk. | **Narrator:** "The longer you train, the more it's yours." |

---

### 5. CLOSE — 15 sec

| VISUAL | AUDIO |
|---|---|
| The person, walking away from the gym, small smile. | **Coach** (one more time, through the AirPods): "Good session. You beat last week's volume by four percent. Same time Thursday?" |
| Cut to black. | *Beat of silence.* |
| Title card, simple, no flourish: **Gym Buddy. The coach everyone should have.** | *(optional)* **Narrator:** "Chapter one is being built now." |

## Production notes

- **Voice of the coach matters more than the visuals.** The whole film lives or dies on whether the coach's voice sounds like a real person with warmth and restraint. Cast accordingly. When production time comes, test with the actual TTS voice we plan to ship (ElevenLabs clone of a specific person, or premium OpenAI voice) so the film and the product feel continuous.
- **Resist the urge to show UI.** One glimpse of a plan adjusting is enough. This is a film about a relationship, not a product tour.
- **No music bed, or very spare.** Gym ambience, breath, the coach's voice. Music will make it feel like an ad; silence makes it feel real.
- **The "one more — push" moment is the film's center of gravity.** If you cut for time, cut elsewhere. That line has to land.
- **Honesty clause.** Nothing depicted here is technically speculative — every element is buildable with 2026 iOS, Apple Watch, AirPods Pro, streaming TTS, and on-device pose. When you show this to friends, you can look them in the eye and say *"every bit of this is real or will be."*

---

## If you need a 30-second cutdown

Use Scene 3 only (The Moment), cold. Open on the set beginning, count, slow, *"one more — push,"* *"that's the one you weren't going to do alone,"* cut to black. Title card. Done.

This is the tightest, highest-punch version — for sending as a clip over text, or opening a pitch.

---

## If a friend asks "is this real?"

Honest answer: the three-exercise MVP shipping in a few weeks nails Scene 3 (the moment). Scenes 1, 2, 4 are the direction we're headed — the morning check-in and the cross-session memory ship in the MVP; the screenless in-ear mode with AirPods + Watch sensor fusion is Chapter 5 of the roadmap, not MVP.

Don't overclaim. Underpromising the timeline and overdelivering the craft is the only way skeptical friends become evangelists.
